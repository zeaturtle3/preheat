Bread Loaf

Hours

Difficulty

Yield

White bread is perfect for soft sandwiches. It is so much more delicious when made from scratch, rather than bought in the store. It’s origins can be traced back to ancient Egypt.

For the dough:

  • 500g white bread flour
  • 1 packet of instant yeast
  • 7g fine salt
  • 30g unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon light corn syrup
  • 11oz lukewarm milk

Step 1
Place the flour, yeast, and salt into a large mixing bowl and mix well. Make a dip in the center, and add the butter, syrup, and milk. Mix everything together with your hand to make a soft dough. If it feels too dry, add milk one tablespoon at a time. If it is too sticky or wet, add a bit more flour.

Step 2
Leave the dough to rest for about 5 minutes, then turn it out onto your workspace. Knead thoroughly for 10 minutes, until silky smooth and pliable without tearing. Return the dough to the bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Allow the dough to rise for 1 hour.

Step 3
Gently punch down the risen dough on a lightly floured work surface. Knead the dough a couple of times to loosen it up, then press the dough down into a 10x12in rectangle. Roll up the dough tightly from the short end, pinching the dough together as you roll. Finally, pinch the seam firmly together, tuck the ends underneath, and place into a greased bread tin.

Step 4
Gently press your hand flat on top of the dough to push it into the corners and to flatten the surface. Cover the tin with plastic wrap, allowing plenty of room for the dough to rise. Let the dough rest for about 1 hour.

Step 5
Towards the end of the rising time, preheat the oven to 425F. Uncover the tin and bake for about 15 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350F and bake for another 20 minutes.

Step 6
