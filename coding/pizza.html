Pizza Crust

Hours

Difficulty

Yield

Pizza - we all know it. Pizza crust is the base of your custom, topping-filled pie. It originates from the Mediterranean, and is
best cooked very hot.

For the dough:

  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon fine salt
  • 1 tablespoon + teaspoon extra-virgin olive oil

For the topping:

  • Your favorite sauce
    • Marinara, alfredo, etc.
  • Your favorite cheeses
    • Mozzarella, parmesan, etc.
  • Your favorite toppings
    • Pepperoni, mushrooms, etc.

Step 1
In a small bowl, stir together the water, maple syrup, and the yeast. Set aside for 5 minutes, until the yeast is foamy.

Step 2
In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook, place the flour and salt. Mix on medium speed until combined. Add the yeast mixture and 1 tablespoon of the olive oil. Mix on medium speed until the dough forms into a ball around the hook, about 5 to 6 minutes.

If the dough is too dry to form a ball, add water 1/2 tablespoon at a time until the mixture comes together. If the dough seems too sticky, add a little more flour.

Step 3
Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead into a smooth ball.

Step 4
Brush a large bowl with the remaining 1 teaspoon of olive oil and place the dough inside. Cover with plastic wrap and set aside to rise until the dough has doubled in size, about 1 hour. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 500F.
