Pita Bread

Hours

Difficulty

Yield

Pita is a yeasted flatbread from the Middle East known for its interior pocket that can hold fillings. It is more crumbly than naan, and often used for wraps.

For the dough:

  • 2 tablespoons active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 cup (35g) whole wheat flour
  • 2 1/2 (310g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 tablspoons olive oil

Step 1
Let’s make the starter: pour 1 cup of lukewarm (100 - 110F) water into a large mixing bowl. Add yeast and sugar, and stir to dissolve. Add the whole wheat flour and 1/4 cup of the all-purpose flour and whisk everything together. Place the bowl in a warm place, uncovered, for about 15 minutes, until it is frothy.

Step 2
Add salt, olive oil and all but 1/2 cup of the remaining all-purpose flour. With a wooden spoon, stir until mixture forms a shaggy ball. Dust with a little flour, then knead in bowl for 1 minute.

Step 3
Turn out dough onto your work surface. Knead lightly for 2 minutes, until smooth. Cover and let rest 10 minutes, then knead again for 2 minutes. Try not to add too much more flour; the dough should be soft and a bit moist.

Step 4
Get another mixing bowl (or clean out the one you just used) and place the dough inside. Cover the bowl tightly with plastic wrap, then cover with a towel. Put the bowl in a warm spot. Leave until dough has doubled in size, about 1 hour.

Step 5
Meanwhile, preheat oven to 475 degrees. On the bottom shelf of the oven, place a baking sheet. Gently punch down dough and divide it into 8 pieces of equal size. Form each piece into a little ball. Place the dough balls on your work surface, cover with a damp towel and leave for 10 minutes.

Step 6
Remove 1 ball, while keeping others covered, and press into a flat disk with rolling pin to an 8-inch diameter.

Step 7
Carefully lift the dough circle and place on the hot baking sheet. After 2 minutes, the dough should be nicely puffed. Turn over with tongs or spatula and bake 1 minute more.
