Naan Bread

Hours

Difficulty

Yield

Naan is a yeasted bread from South Asia traditionally baked in a tandoor oven. It is soft and fluffy, and typically paired with curries.

For the dough:

  • 1 1/2 cups (180g) all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup (90g) bread flour
  • 1/2 cup + 2 tablespoons (142g) warm water
  • 5 tablespoons (71g) plain, full-fat Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons (28g) melted ghee or butter
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the topping:

  • 2 tablespoons (28g) melted ghee or butter
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

Step 1
In the large bowl of a stand mixer, combine all the dough ingredients and mix until shaggy.

Step 2
Knead the dough until smooth, bouncy, and slightly tacky.

knead some help?

Step 3
Divide the dough into 8 equal pieces, about 65g each. Shape each piece into a ball, cover. Let rest for 20 minutes.

Step 4
While the dough is resting, preheat a griddle or pan over medium-high heat.

Step 5
On a lightly floured surface, stretch (or use a rolling pin) to roll each ball into a 6” round or 7” oblong. Be sure to keep the other pieces covered while working!

Step 6
Once the pan or griddle is hot, cook the naan for 30 to 40 seconds, until bubbles form on the top side and the underside brown. Flip it over to brown the other side for 40 to 50 seconds, checking regularly to see if it’s ready.
